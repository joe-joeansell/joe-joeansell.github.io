CareBo - Interaction Design Focused Proof-Of-Concept For A Mental Health Robot

2025 University Project. A physical prototype for a robot that explores the process of mental health through tangible and gamified design.

Interaction Design | Graphic Design/Concept Art | Team Building & Co-Ordination | Human-Computer Interaction/Tangible UI

This was a group project, created with two other University students. The brief was to explore Interaction Design principles through the process of creating an interactive prototype. To balance our group's skills, we took a lo-fi approach and focused on making a 'wizard of oz' style prototype, creating a guided experience to demonstrate a hypothetical future interaction.

In this case, the 'future interaction' was in the form of CareBo, a robotic device that uses design principles from Mental Healthcare apps and interactive pet toys such as Tamagotchi or Furby. The goal was to allow users to explore their mental health, and their relationships and treatment of others through the CareBo device, such as earning 'tokens' for positive tasks which could be fed to CareBo.
