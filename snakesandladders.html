Snakes & Ladders & You - Exploring Accessible And Tangible User Interaction Via Board Games

2025 University Project. An prototype version of Snakes And Ladders designed to be more tactile and accessible for a wide range of players. Built with Arduino.

Interaction Design | Team Building & Co-Ordination | Human-Computer Interaction/Tangible UI | Arduino Electronics/Coding

This was a group project, created with three other University students. The brief was to explore Interaction Design principles through the creation of a physical artefact that made use of Arduino components and software. We chose to develop on our work with TUIs on CareBo, and decided to create a version of the board game 'Snakes and Ladders', using Arduino components to emphasize accessible and tangible methods of play.

Our project came together as two separate pieces that come together to create the experience. The first is a 'Dice' container, which contains an OLED screen and a sound sensor. When players speak into the sound sensor, it will display a corresponding roll on the screen. Players then take their roll and enact it on the game board. When they touch their piece to either a 'Snake' space or a 'Ladder' space, it will make a light turn on and vibrate the board, providing visual feedback and also tactile feedback for those with visual impairments.

On this project, I was primarily responsible for the coding of the 'dice' component, as well as being heavily involved in the physical construction of our artefact. The pieces used to construct our artefact, such as the acrylic faceplate for the board, were designed and laser cut by me.
